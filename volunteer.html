As President

Duties &
responsibilities

Chairing meetings

Setting the agenda for monthly general meetings and leading the executive committee through club business and project reviews.

Service project leadership

Sponsoring and overseeing the club's community service calendar — from plantation drives to health camps — with clear ownership on every project.

District & zone representation

Representing Midtown Club at district and zone Innerwheel conferences, and liaising with district leadership on club standing and initiatives.

Member mentorship

Onboarding new members and encouraging existing ones into committee and leadership roles of their own.

Budget & reporting

Overseeing the club's budget and fundraising targets, with periodic reporting to district Innerwheel leadership.

Community partnerships

Building relationships with local schools, old age homes, and orphanages that the club partners with on ongoing initiatives.

Why It Matters

Community work,
professional habits

Leading with accountability

Chairing a club and reporting to district leadership builds the same stakeholder communication muscle used with executive management in finance work.

Coordination at scale

Running plantation and donation drives builds the same event-logistics discipline that later shows up in coordinating multi-entity reporting cycles.

Resource stewardship

Fundraising campaigns require the same accountability for every rupee that statutory audit work demands.

Clear communication

Awareness campaigns are an exercise in translating a cause into a message people act on — not unlike turning a dashboard into a decision.
